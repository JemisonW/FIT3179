Reducing Risks On The Road

A Cursory Look Into How To Stay Safe On Victorian Roads

Driving is statistically one of the most dangerous things you will ever do in your life and results in hundreds of fatalities in Victoria every year.
Despite the lower population, many council ares have a high number of accidents due to the large number of traffic that travels through them.

Do you know your safety?

What about Motorbikes?

While getting behind the wheel of a car can seem dangerous, there are a number of safety features in place to protect you.
Motorcyclists aren't as lucky, in the event of a crash they are 15% more likely to be Seriously Injured or Killed.

You may be aware that speed is a massive factor in the outcome of a crash, but do you know just how much?
If you have an accident driving at 90km/h, compared to 80km/h, the risk of death doubles.

SLOW DOWN!

Even the day of the week affects the likelihood of you getting in an accident. On average, 2000 more accidents per day during the week compared to the weekend.

Getting Away For The Weekend

The time of year also has a big impact on our road toll. The summer months are by far the most dangerous time to drive and winter is the safest time, despite the poor driving conditions it presents.
